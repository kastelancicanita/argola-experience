Activities & the Area

  • 7 km of beaches and seaside promenade from Mutogras to Strožanac — ideal for walking, jogging, and cycling by the sea.
  • Perun Hill nearby — hiking trails and scenic walks above Podstrana.
  • Church of St. George (Crkva sv. Jure), over 1,000 years old — historic site above Gornja Podstrana.
  • Gornja Podstrana — sweeping views of the sea, the islands, and Split.
  • Water sports — we recommend Argola Experience (jet ski, sea safari, and more; see above).

Island links from Split (distances)

Ferries and catamarans from Split connect Brač (~20 km), Šolta (~25 km), Hvar (~40 km), and Vis (~55 km). Allow about 20 minutes by car from the apartment to Split Ferry Port.

What to visit

Favourite day trips, roughly ordered from closest to furthest — Omiš, Split, and Trogir are easy to reach by car or public transport.

Omiš

Where the mountains, river, and sea meet

  • Starigrad (Fortica) & Mirabela fortress — panoramic views
  • Cetina River Canyon — one of Croatia’s most beautiful gorges
  • Rafting on the Cetina — one of Omiš’s signature activities
  • Hinterland: Church of St. George in Gata, Mila Gojsalić monument
  • Canyoning, ziplines, and hiking
  • Hidden coves — best by rented boat or kayak

Split

A lively Mediterranean city full of history

  • Diocletian’s Palace & the Old Town (UNESCO)
  • Cathedral of St. Domnius & the Peristyle — climb the bell tower
  • Riva — the iconic waterfront
  • Marjan Hill — walking trails and views over the islands
  • Meštrović Gallery

Trogir

About 42 km (roughly 45–60 min by car)

  • UNESCO-listed “town-museum” on a small island
  • Cathedral of St. Lawrence & Radovan’s portal
  • Kamerlengo fortress & the waterfront

Deeper day trips (van-friendly)

These are further from Podstrana — easiest with a car or a van you can book through us. For transfers and vehicle rental, use Transport, taxi & van (Paulo) at the top of this section.

1. Klis Fortress & ancient Salona

History without filters (and a famous filming location)

Only about 20 minutes from Podstrana. Combine Klis — the best viewpoint over Split — with the vast Roman site of Salona. Ideal for a half-day or afternoon.

2. Krka National Park (Slapovi Krke)

Instagram vs. reality (reality is still amazing)

About one hour’s drive. Perfect for a full day away from the beach crowds. Skradinski buk is a must. We can drop you so you are not stuck circling for parking.

3. Imotski lakes (Blue & Red Lake)

Natural drama inland

About one hour, through the Sveti Ilija tunnel. Blue Lake for swimming (if you are up for the path down); Red Lake for a jaw-dropping view. On the way: some of the best pršut and cheese in Zagora.

4. Skywalk Biokovo (Nebeska šetnica)

A view that makes your legs tingle

About 1.5 hours. A glass walkway at 1,228 m, with the Makarska riviera at your feet — hold your phone tight.

5. Plitvice Lakes National Park

Yes, it is far. Yes, it is worth it.

About 2.5–3 hours. Croatia’s most famous lakes. Best as an early start: nap in the van on the way, back in Podstrana in time for dinner.
